Self-review — Corporate Identity / Premium Email Design / Typography / Gmail Compatibility

  • ✓ Logo strength (Corporate Identity): 90 px on the CDC badge is a meaningful step up from 72 px. At 90 px the mark occupies enough visual mass to read as the primary brand anchor. The identity block (name at 21 px bold + two 13 px lines) sits beside it without overrunning it. The logo now grounds the left zone — the name reads as a label within the brand, not a competing headline.
  • ✓ Logo vs. name balance: 21 px name bold at roughly 28–30 px rendered line height is visually subordinate to the 90 px logo mass. The logo is wider and its rendered height (driven by badge proportions) is comparable to the three-line stack height, creating a natural two-zone horizontal balance. The employee name does not overpower the brand mark at these proportions.
  • ✓ Award banner harmony (Premium Email Design): 202 px is a meaningful reduction from 248 px (full) and sits closer in visual weight to the three-line identity block. The award reads as a credential, not a marketing panel. FT/Statista text inside the badge remains legible at 202 px in a browser; real Gmail rendering will be similar. Not distorted — original image proportions are preserved (width-only resize, height: auto).
  • ✓ Typography (Typography Designer): 21 / 13 / 13 px three-level hierarchy is clean. Name at 21 px bold in #1a1a2e is clearly the primary text element. Title at 13 px regular in #555555 steps back. Company line at 13 px semibold in brand orange #F16623 is the warmest element — bridges text and brand color without competing with the logo or name. 5 px name-to-title gap and 3 px title-to-company gap give a tight but readable stack. Contact text at 13 px matches the identity secondary lines — consistent secondary scale across the signature. Disclaimer at 10 px / 1.45 line-height is clearly tertiary, comfortable to scan, not cramped.
  • ✓ Compact feel remains premium: 580 px is positioned between the optimized compact (560 px) and compact (600 px). The 15 px top/bottom padding on ROW 1 (vs 14 px in optimized-compact) gives slightly more breathing room around the increased logo size. The overall signature feels present without dominating the email body. Contact row at 10 px vertical padding scans cleanly. The thin 1 px #e0e0e0 dividers keep the layout architecturally restrained.
  • ✓ Disclaimer (Gmail Compatibility): 10 px / line-height 1.45 / color #666666 / text-align left (not justified). Full corporate text is present word for word. Reads clearly as legal boilerplate without appearing broken or clipped. Gmail may round to 10 px exactly — acceptable.
  • ✓ Gmail compatibility: All CSS inline. No style blocks, no web fonts, no base64, no SVG inline elements. table-layout: fixed with explicit width attributes on all columns. bgcolor attributes on all cells. 1 px divider and pipe columns use bgcolor (not CSS border). All image URLs are public HTTPS (GitHub Pages). colspan="6" matches all six columns in the layout.
  • ✓ Verdict: This 580 px variant strikes the intended balance — the CDC logo now properly anchors the brand zone, the employee name is subordinate to the mark, the award credential is proportionate and readable, and the overall scale feels premium rather than compressed. Recommended as the preferred daily-use variant over the 560 px optimized-compact.
