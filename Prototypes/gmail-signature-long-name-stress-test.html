Stress Test Findings — Generator Logic Recommendations

Name wrapping — observed behavior

  • A (Anna Semenova, 13 chars): Fits one line comfortably at 21 px. No wrapping.
  • E (Muhammad Usman, 14 chars): Fits one line comfortably at 21 px. No wrapping.
  • B (Bruno Marçal Vieira Manzato, ~27 chars): Wraps at 21 px in a 259 px column. Browser renders approx 22–24 chars per line at 21 px bold Arial. Wraps cleanly at a word boundary — layout remains balanced.
  • C (Adriany Domingues de Oliveira, ~29 chars): Wraps at 21 px. Clean break. Short title compensates for added row height.
  • D (Christoffel Francois Viljoen, ~28 chars): Wraps at 21 px. "Christoffel" is a very wide first word; line 1 likely "Christoffel" only, line 2 "Francois Viljoen". Layout holds.
  • F (Alexander Constantin Alexandrovich, ~34 chars): Wraps at 21 px — likely 2 lines. Title also wraps (49 chars at 13 px). Identity stack becomes 5–6 lines. Row is noticeably taller but does not overflow or break the fixed 580 px width. The floating divider re-centers automatically to the taller row height. Award banner stays aligned via valign="middle". Layout holds but is no longer "compact" for this person.

Title wrapping — observed behavior

  • Short titles (A, C, D, E short version): fit one line at 13 px in 259 px column.
  • E (Environment & Sustainability Manager, ~36 chars): Borderline — likely wraps to 2 lines at 13 px regular Arial (~34 chars/line capacity). Adds one extra text line to the identity stack.
  • F (Senior International Business Development Manager, ~49 chars): Definitely wraps to 2 lines. Combined with a wrapping name, this is the hardest case.

Layout stability under wrapping

  • The fixed 580 px table width is maintained in all cases — table-layout: fixed prevents any column from expanding. Text wraps within the identity cell only.
  • The logo cell height is set by the image (104 px wide, height: auto). If the identity stack grows taller than the logo cell, the row height is driven by the identity column instead. In that case the logo aligns to the top of the taller cell (valign="middle" will center it vertically within the cell).
  • The floating divider re-centers automatically — its nested 64 px line centers itself within whatever the computed row height becomes. This is a structural advantage over a full-height separator.
  • The award banner also re-aligns via valign="middle" in the award cell. Award image height is proportional to 184 px width; if the row becomes taller from text wrapping, the award floats vertically centered.

Recommended generator rules

  • Name ≤ 22 chars: use 21 px. Fits one line. No adjustment needed.
  • Name 23–30 chars: use 21 px. Wraps to 2 lines — acceptable. The row remains visually balanced if the title is short. Flag in generator preview.
  • Name > 30 chars: consider reducing name to 19–20 px to attempt fitting on one line, or accept 2-line wrapping and reduce row padding from 14 px to 10 px to compensate. Flag in generator as "long name — review signature height."
  • Title ≤ 32 chars: fits one line at 13 px. No adjustment needed.
  • Title 33–42 chars: wraps to 2 lines at 13 px. Acceptable. Flag in generator preview.
  • Title > 42 chars: wraps to 2+ lines. Consider reducing title font to 12 px or asking the user to shorten the title. Flag as "very long title."
  • Combined: long name + long title: this layout (example F) is visually functional but no longer compact. Generator should warn: "This signature is longer than recommended for compact daily use. Consider shortening the title or using an abbreviated name."
  • Table width: always stays at 580 px regardless of name/title length. No generator logic needed for width.

Copy safety

  • Each signature block is the first and only element inside its .preview-chrome div. The div's padding (22 px 26 px) is CSS-only — it does not produce HTML whitespace that would be pasted into Gmail. Selecting from inside the white card boundary and copying into Gmail captures only the table HTML.
  • The page header, labels, observation notes, and this findings section are entirely outside the .preview-chrome divs and cannot be accidentally pasted into Gmail if the user selects only within a card.
